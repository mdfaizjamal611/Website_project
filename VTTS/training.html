NDT Personnel Training & Certification as per SNT-TC-1A / ISO 9712

1. Standards: SNT-TC-1A and ISO 9712 guide NDT personnel training and certification globally.
2. Certification Levels: Levels I, II, and III are assigned based on proficiency in specific NDT methods.
3. Training Requirements: Comprehensive programs cover theory, practical skills, and relevant standards.
4. Experience: Candidates must gain hands-on experience, documented for verification.
5. Examinations: Both written and practical exams assess theoretical understanding and practical skills.
6. Certification Body: Authorized bodies, internal or external, grant certifications meeting standard requirements.
7. Recertification: Periodic renewal ensures ongoing competence through additional training and testing.
8. Quality Assurance: Maintaining a quality assurance program is crucial for consistent and reliable NDT practices.
9. International Recognition: ISO 9712 provides a globally accepted basis for NDT personnel certification.
10. Professional Development: Continuous learning is encouraged for NDT personnel to stay current with industry advancements.

Awareness Course in ISO 9001; 14001 & 45001

1. ISO Standards Overview: Core principles of ISO 9001 (Quality Management), ISO 14001 (Environmental Management), and ISO 45001 (Occupational Health and Safety).
2. Quality Management System (QMS): Requirements and benefits of ISO 9001 for effective quality management within organizations.
3. Environmental Management System (EMS): Insights into ISO 14001 for developing sustainable environmental practices.
4. Occupational Health and Safety Management System (OHSMS): Principles of ISO 45001, focusing on ensuring a safe and healthy workplace.
5. Integration of Systems: Strategies for synergizing QMS, EMS, and OHSMS within an organization.
6. Risk-Based Thinking: Importance of risk assessment in ISO standards for enhanced decision-making.
7. Compliance and Legal Requirements: How ISO standards aid organizations in meeting legal and regulatory obligations.
8. Internal Auditing: Insights into conducting internal audits to assess management system effectiveness.
9. Continuous Improvement: Emphasis on continual improvement as a fundamental aspect of ISO standards.
10. Employee Engagement: Employee role in successful ISO implementation and fostering a culture of quality, environmental responsibility, and safety awareness.

Internal Auditors in ISO 9001, 14001 & 45001

1. Crucial Role of Internal Auditors: Assessing and verifying compliance with ISO 9001, ISO 14001, and ISO 45001 standards.
2. Competency Requirements: Auditors need skills, knowledge, and training for effective evaluation of management systems.
3. Independence and Impartiality: Ensuring unbiased and fair evaluations, maintaining independence.
4. Thorough Audit Planning: Defining scope, objectives, and criteria, selecting appropriate methods.
5. Risk-Based Approach: Adopting a risk-focused strategy, prioritizing areas with higher impacts on quality, environment, and safety.
6. Documentation Review: Comprehensive review of documentation for alignment with ISO standards.
7. Interviews and Observation: Interaction and observation to gather valuable insights into process effectiveness.
8. Nonconformity Identification: Identifying deviations from standards and areas for improvement.
9. Clear Reporting: Providing concise reports outlining findings, nonconformities, and improvement opportunities.
10. Contribution to Improvement: Internal auditors enhance organizational effectiveness by offering feedback and recommendations for continual improvement.

Guided Tour of ASME Codes

1. Introduction to ASME Codes: Overview of the American Society of Mechanical Engineers (ASME) codes, which set standards for the design, fabrication, and inspection of mechanical components and systems.
2. Code Sections and Divisions: Exploration of key sections and divisions within ASME codes, such as Section I for Power Boilers, Section VIII for Pressure Vessels, and Section IX for Welding and Brazing Qualifications.
3. Code Compliance and Certification: Understanding the process of ensuring compliance with ASME codes, including certification requirements and the role of Authorized Inspection Agencies (AIAs).
4. Materials and Design Criteria: Insight into the materials specifications and design criteria outlined in ASME codes, addressing factors such as material selection, stress analysis, and safety margins.
5. Case Studies and Applications: Practical examples and case studies illustrating how ASME codes are applied in various industries, emphasizing their role in ensuring safety, reliability, and compliance with regulatory standards.

Welders & Welding Operators Training

1. Basic Welding Techniques: Training covers fundamental welding techniques, including arc welding, gas welding, and electric welding, ensuring a solid foundation for welders and welding operators.
2. Safety Protocols and Procedures: Emphasis on safety measures, personal protective equipment (PPE), and adherence to industry standards to create a secure working environment during welding operations.
3. Material Preparation and Inspection: Instruction on the proper preparation of materials for welding, including cleaning, cutting, and fitting, coupled with guidance on inspecting materials to meet quality standards.
4. Welding Codes and Standards: Familiarization with relevant welding codes and standards, such as AWS (American Welding Society) codes, ensuring compliance with industry regulations and the ability to produce welds of the desired quality.
5. Hands-On Practical Training: Practical, hands-on training sessions allowing welders and welding operators to apply theoretical knowledge, refine their skills, and gain experience in various welding processes and positions.

Welding Inspectors’ Training Course

1. Welding Processes and Techniques: Comprehensive training on various welding processes (e.g., arc welding, gas welding) and techniques to enable inspectors to assess weld quality and adherence to standards.
2. Understanding Welding Symbols and Drawings: Instruction on interpreting welding symbols and blueprints, ensuring welding inspectors can accurately assess the specifications and requirements of a welding project.
3. Inspection Methods and Equipment: Training on non-destructive testing (NDT) methods, visual inspection, and the use of inspection equipment such as ultrasonic testing devices and X-ray machines to identify defects and ensure weld integrity.
4. Code Compliance and Standards: In-depth knowledge of welding codes and standards, including ASME, AWS, and other relevant industry specifications, to assess compliance and verify that welding work meets regulatory requirements.
5. Documentation and Reporting: Guidance on maintaining accurate records, generating inspection reports, and communicating findings effectively to stakeholders, ensuring transparency and accountability in the welding inspection process.

1. Introduction to NDT Methods: Overview of common Non-Destructive Testing methods, including Radiography, Ultrasonics, Magnetic Particle Testing, Liquid Penetrant Testing, and Visual Inspection.
2. Benefits and Applications: Understanding the advantages of NDT in detecting defects without damaging the material, with applications across industries such as manufacturing, construction, and aerospace.
3. Safety Considerations: Emphasis on safety protocols during NDT activities to protect personnel and ensure the integrity of the testing process.
4. Regulatory Compliance: Awareness of relevant industry standards and regulations governing NDT, ensuring that testing activities align with quality and safety requirements.
5. Role in Quality Assurance: Recognition of NDT's critical role in quality assurance, emphasizing its contribution to ensuring the reliability and longevity of materials and structures.

4. Quality Assurance Integration: corporation of NDT as a quality assurance tool during the design phase, emphasizing the role of NDT in ensuring the integrity and durability of the final product.
5. Collaboration with NDT Specialists: Fostering collaboration between designers and NDT specialists to promote a holistic approach to design, where considerations for testing and inspection are integral to the overall design process.
